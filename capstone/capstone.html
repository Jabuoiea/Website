ABSTRACT
In today’s present society, the use of digital devices appears to take up most of a person's daily life as the vast majority of human activities such as paying bills, buying food and supplies, and performing tasks at work can be done by clicking the mouse or tapping on the screen. It has become apparent, however, that this dependence on digital devices has caused people to spend more time in virtual space and less time for actual human-world interaction–an observation highlighted particularly in the midst of the Covid -19 pandemic. To raise awareness on this issue, Re:CONNECT, a short animated film, was produced as part of a campaign to encourage people to reconnect with others through meaningful conversations while living in a highly digitally connected world. Data gathered through interviews became the basis for the animated film’s content. The proponents of this capstone project foresee people will go into a digital detox from time to time to focus on real-life social interactions without digital distractions: to reconnect.

Keywords: animation, disconnection, social commentary, and reconnection.
